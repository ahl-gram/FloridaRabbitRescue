# Rescue Organization Outreach Email

## Notes on sending

- Send individually — not as a mass email. Each rescue should feel like a personal outreach, not a blast.
- Use your project email address (e.g. hello@floridarabbitrescues.org), not a personal Gmail.
- Customize the [RESCUE NAME] and any specific detail you know about them.
- Send *before* the site goes live — this gives them a chance to correct anything before the public sees it.
- Log the date sent and any responses received (see launch-checklist.md).

---

## Email Template

**Subject:** Florida Rabbit Rescue Directory — listing your organization, seeking your input

---

Hi [RESCUE NAME] team,

My name is [YOUR NAME], and I'm building a free, publicly accessible directory of rabbit rescue organizations in Florida — called the Florida Rabbit Rescue Directory.

The goal is simple: to help Florida residents who are considering adopting a rabbit find reputable rescue organizations, and to introduce them to the values and care philosophy laid out by House Rabbit Society (houserabbit.org) and rabbit.org Foundation before they bring a rabbit home. I believe the more people understand what responsible rabbit ownership looks like — indoor housing, spay/neuter, exotic veterinary care, long-term commitment — the better outcomes there are for rabbits and the people who adopt them.

**[RESCUE NAME] is included in the directory**, and I wanted to reach out before the site goes live to make sure the information I've compiled is accurate.

Here is what I currently have listed for your organization:

- **Mission statement:** [PASTE THEIR MISSION STATEMENT FROM directory-data.json]
- **Founded:** [YEAR]
- **Service area:** [SERVICE AREA]
- **501(c)(3) status:** [STATUS]
- **Board of Directors:** [BOARD STATUS NOTE]
- **Website:** [URL]

If anything above is incorrect, incomplete, or if you'd like to update your mission statement to reflect how you describe your organization today, I'd be grateful for your input. I'm also happy to add any details that would be helpful to prospective adopters.

A few things I want to be clear about:

- This directory is **free** — there is no charge to be listed and I'm not selling anything.
- I am **not affiliated** with any rescue organization, House Rabbit Society, or rabbit.org Foundation. This is an independent project.
- The directory does **not rank or score** organizations. It presents verified, objective facts and lets visitors decide for themselves.
- I plan to maintain and update this at least annually.

Once the site is live, I'll send you a link to your listing directly. If you're open to it, I'd also love it if you'd consider sharing the directory with your community — the more visibility it gets, the more prospective adopters it can reach before they make uninformed decisions.

Thank you for the work you do for rabbits in Florida. Please feel free to reply to this email with any questions, corrections, or feedback.

Warm regards,

[YOUR NAME]
[PROJECT EMAIL]
[WEBSITE URL — once live]
